journal_eight(statue_of_liberty) {

The site I chose for journal 8 represents a good attempt at a clean visual hierarchy but misses a lot of Steve Krug's points. I chose StatueOfLiberty.org because it seems like a great candidate for a usability test. Even with its flaws, it wouldn't take much to polish it into a great website.

}

'Statue of Liberty's Purpose

At first glance - and going off of little more than the website name - StatueOfLiberty.org seems to be a site solely dedicated to information about the monument. That being said, a quick scan of the home page identifies the word "Foundation" used several times. A welcome blurb near the middle of the page clearly states the sites purpose of restoring and preserving the monument. Below that, a link welcomes users to place a donation.

StatueOfLiberty.org has an interesting problem where its URL doesn't seem to describe what the site is about. This might throw people off at first, but I believe the home page does enough to guide people in the right direction.

Critique

StatueOfLiberty.org does a great job of providing useful content to people. It is easy to understand and all of the links do what you would expect them to do. In this regard, there is very little wrong with the site. Visually, however, the site breaks many web conventions that could easily confuse someone new to the Internet.

The site has a site ID but it is very small and hidden in the header image.

The site also has primary navigation in the form of tabs (which Krug highly recommends) but the tabs expand to a welcome blurb instead of secondary navigation links. On some pages, they don't expand at all.

The site has some secondary navigation links midway down the page; however, they are in a vertical column in the middle of the page (not pushed up against one of the edges of the site) and are easy to miss.

Lastly, the site attempts to use a layout that scales well to screens of any size, which is good, but I don't believe the approach is very successful. The layout features all of the content on the left half of the screen. On the right is simply a grey rectangle.

Potential Improvements

First, I would change the layout. I would probably put all of the content in a wide div in the center of the screen that scales horizontally with any screen (essentially centering the content) and I would have two grey columns on either side.

Next, I would change the persistent navigation to feature a site ID (simply removing the header image and taking the site ID out and enlarging it). I would also add a basic tagline.

Then, I would change the primary navigation (the tabs) to expand to either the relevant secondary navigation links (the ones currently in the middle of the page) or to a welcome blurb. Either way, it should be consistent throughout the site.
